NEVER add a "LinkedIn audience reframe" that redirects giveaways away from the client's actual customers toward industry peers/operators. This was a major error.

**Why:** The user pointed out that a dietitian's LinkedIn audience is patients, not other dietitians. The system was misreading the "Broad > Niche at TOF" rule (from Linear's playbook) as "broaden the AUDIENCE to B2B operators" when it actually means "broaden the TOPIC within your target audience." Linear's context is B2B agency-to-brand — that doesn't generalize.

**How to apply:**
- The audience for the giveaway = the client's actual customers, unless the client explicitly says otherwise
- "Broad > Niche" means pick widely relevant TOPICS within that audience, not reframe WHO the audience is
- A dietitian targeting patients → giveaways about gut health FOR patients
- A realtor targeting buyers → giveaways about buying homes FOR buyers
- A DTC brand founder building a personal brand among OTHER founders → THEN the audience is operators (but only because the client said so)
- When in doubt, the audience is whoever the client sells to
