Lead magnet ideas and titles MUST feel like stolen internal assets, not generic content marketing suggestions. The user was unhappy when proposals read like blog post titles ("The Scottsdale Luxury Market Report", "The Insider's Checklist").

**Why:** Linear's giveaways work because they sound like real tools being shared from behind the curtain — "Our Top 9 Copywriting Frameworks", "470+ High-Impact DTC Creative Hooks", "Customer Generated Content SOP". The titles are outcome-denominated (anchored to revenue, results, volume), proprietary-framed ("Our", "The Exact"), and format-labeled. They feel like stealing from an agency's internal drive.

**How to apply:** Before presenting any idea, run it through the Insider Artifact Test (now in core-reference.md Section 5):
1. Does it sound stolen from behind the curtain? (possessive framing + real results)
2. Is value denominated in outcomes? (credential numbers, not page counts)
3. Would a competitor pay money for it? (proprietary data/process, not repackaged generic advice)

Bad: "The Scottsdale Luxury Market Report" → Good: "Our $150M+ in Closed Luxury Sales, Exposed: Scottsdale Neighborhood Benchmarks"
Bad: "The Insider's Checklist — 27 Questions" → Good: "27 Questions We Ask Before Any $2M+ Purchase (Most Agents Skip 20 of Them)"
