# Connect

> **Verb:** Access people & exclusivity | **Goal:** Belonging, FOMO

## Definition
Connect lead magnets trade an email address for access to people, conversations, and exclusive spaces — leveraging the human need to belong and the fear of missing out.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Nurture (post-initial conversion, ongoing relationship)
- **Business types:** Brands with strong community potential, personal brands, membership-based businesses, industry leaders with active audiences
- **Audience types:** People who value networking, peer learning, and insider access over static content
- **Goals:** Long-term relationship building, recurring engagement, brand loyalty, reducing churn, creating advocates

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Slack Communities** | Private workspace with topic channels | High (ongoing) | Your audience is professional and already uses Slack |
| **Discord Communities** | Server with channels, roles, and bots | High (ongoing) | Your audience skews younger, tech-savvy, or creator-oriented |
| **Facebook Groups** | Private group within Facebook's ecosystem | Medium (ongoing) | Your audience is active on Facebook and prefers low-friction access |
| **Membership Libraries** | Gated content hubs with exclusive resources | High | You produce enough content to justify ongoing access |
| **Workshops** | Live, interactive sessions focused on a skill or topic | Medium | You want high-touch engagement with a smaller group |
| **Masterminds** | Small-group peer advisory sessions | High | Your audience values peer accountability and strategic thinking |
| **Office Hours** | Drop-in Q&A sessions with an expert | Low | You want recurring touchpoints without heavy prep |
| **AMAs (Ask Me Anything)** | Open Q&A events, often with a guest expert | Low | You want to borrow authority from a guest or create event buzz |
| **Early / Beta Access** | First look at new products, features, or content | Low | You're launching something and want engaged early adopters |
| **Curated Newsletters** | Regular email with exclusive insights, not available on blog | Medium (ongoing) | You can consistently deliver value that's worth the inbox space |

## Linear Examples
None — this is a gap in Linear's current library. Community-based lead magnets represent an untapped opportunity, particularly:
- A **Creative Strategist Slack Community** — private space for DTC creative leads to share wins, ask questions, and access Linear's team directly
- **Monthly Creative Teardown Office Hours** — live sessions where Linear's team analyzes submitted ads
- **Beta Access to New Frameworks** — early access to Linear's latest templates and tools before public release

These formats would deepen relationships with existing leads and turn one-time downloaders into an engaged audience.

## When NOT to Use
- **You can't commit to maintaining the community.** A dead Slack channel or an abandoned Discord is worse than no community at all. Connect magnets require ongoing investment in moderation, content, and engagement. Budget for at least 5-10 hours per week.
- **Your audience doesn't value networking.** Some audiences are pure practitioners — they want the tool, the template, the answer. They don't want to chat about it. Test demand before building infrastructure.
- **You need quick, one-time lead capture.** Community is a long game. If your goal is "get 500 emails this week," a cheat sheet will outperform a Slack invite. Connect magnets build slowly but compound over time.
- **You don't have a critical mass of members.** An empty community repels new members. Have a launch plan that seeds the group with at least 50-100 active people before opening it as a lead magnet.
