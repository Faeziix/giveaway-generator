# Educate

> **Verb:** Learn | **Goal:** Build authority through teaching

## Definition
Educate lead magnets transfer knowledge to the prospect, positioning your brand as the trusted expert on a topic they care about.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Awareness
- **Business types:** Agencies, consultancies, SaaS companies, coaches — anyone whose value proposition starts with expertise
- **Audience types:** People new to a topic, researching a problem, or looking to level up a skill
- **Goals:** Thought leadership, top-of-funnel volume, brand awareness, nurturing cold audiences

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Ebooks / Guides** | Long-form written deep dives on a single topic | High | You have original insights worth 2,000+ words |
| **Whitepapers** | Data-backed, formal reports — often PDF | High | B2B audience that values research rigor |
| **Reports** | Industry trends, survey results, annual reviews | High | You have proprietary or aggregated data |
| **Articles / Blog posts** | Gated or ungated written content, shorter than ebooks | Low | Quick wins; testing a topic before committing to long-form |
| **Cheat sheets** | One-page reference docs — condensed, scannable | Low | The topic has rules, steps, or specs people forget |
| **Playbooks** | Step-by-step tactical guides for a specific workflow | Medium | Your audience needs a repeatable process |
| **Infographics** | Visual summaries of data, processes, or comparisons | Medium | The concept is easier to show than tell |
| **Flowcharts / Diagrams** | Decision trees, system maps, process flows | Medium | Choices branch based on conditions |
| **Slide decks / Carousels** | Sequenced visual frames — great for social | Medium | Repurposing a talk or breaking down a framework |
| **Webinars** | Live or recorded presentations, usually 30-60 min | High | You want real-time engagement + Q&A |
| **Mini-courses** | Multi-lesson sequences delivered over days/weeks | High | The topic is too big for a single asset |
| **Tutorials** | Screenshares, walkthroughs, how-to videos | Medium | The skill is procedural — "watch me do it" |
| **Podcast episodes** | Audio deep dives, interviews, panel discussions | Medium | Your audience consumes content on the go |
| **Video breakdowns** | Short analyses of examples, ads, strategies | Medium | Learning by example is more effective than theory |
| **Resource lists** | Curated collections of tools, links, or references | Low | Saving the audience hours of research |
| **Reading lists** | Book or article recommendations, annotated | Low | You want to borrow authority from other experts |
| **Tool stacks** | "Here's what we use and why" breakdowns | Low | Your audience is evaluating tools |
| **Directories** | Categorized lists of vendors, agencies, communities | Medium | The space is fragmented and hard to navigate |

## Linear Examples
- **In-depth Training on 4 Core ROAS Metrics** — Video format; teaches a foundational concept that most of Linear's audience misunderstands or underuses.
- **How to Build Your Own AI Creative Strategist** — Tutorial; positions Linear at the intersection of AI and creative strategy, a timely authority play.
- **Whitelisting Cheat Sheet** — One-page reference; distills a complex platform-specific process into a scannable doc.
- **Unlimited Ad Variations Cheat Sheet** — One-page reference; gives a repeatable method prospects can pin to their wall.
- **Q4 Creative Strategy Cheatsheet** — Timely seasonal guide; combines urgency with education.

## When NOT to Use
- **Your audience already knows the topic.** Experienced practitioners want tools and templates, not another "intro to X." Educating experts feels patronizing.
- **The topic is too broad or generic.** "The Ultimate Guide to Marketing" attracts everyone and converts no one. Narrow the scope until the title makes a specific promise.
- **You need high-intent leads.** Education attracts browsers and researchers. If your sales team needs decision-stage leads, pair educate assets with a stronger bottom-of-funnel offer.
- **You can't say something original.** If your ebook reads like a rewrite of the top 5 Google results, it erodes trust instead of building it.
