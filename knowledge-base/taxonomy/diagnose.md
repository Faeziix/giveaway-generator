# Diagnose

> **Verb:** Understand their situation | **Goal:** Personalized insight

## Definition
Diagnose lead magnets help the prospect evaluate where they stand — revealing gaps, strengths, and next steps specific to their situation.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Awareness (quizzes are viral), Consideration (scorecards qualify intent)
- **Business types:** Consultancies, agencies, coaches, SaaS companies with complex products — anyone whose sales process starts with "it depends"
- **Audience types:** People who suspect they have a problem but aren't sure how big it is, or where to start fixing it
- **Goals:** Lead segmentation, high engagement rates, collecting qualifying data, viral sharing (quizzes), booking discovery calls

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Personality-style Quizzes** | "What type of X are you?" — sorts users into archetypes | Medium | You want virality and top-of-funnel volume |
| **Knowledge Tests** | "How well do you know X?" — scores understanding | Low | You want to reveal knowledge gaps that your offer fills |
| **Scorecards** | "Rate your X on 10 dimensions" — produces a score | Medium | You want a structured way to quantify a prospect's situation |
| **Audits** | "Grade your strategy / website / funnel" — detailed evaluation | High | Your sales process naturally starts with an audit |
| **Maturity Models** | "What stage is your X at?" — maps to a progression framework | Medium | Your audience's needs differ dramatically by maturity level |
| **Readiness Checklists** | "Are you ready for X?" — binary yes/no checklist | Low | You want to qualify whether someone is a fit for your offer |
| **Benchmarking Tools** | "How do you compare to others?" — peer comparison | High | You have aggregate data to make the comparison meaningful |
| **Gap Analysis Surveys** | "Where are the gaps?" — identifies discrepancies between current and ideal state | Medium | The prospect needs to see the distance between where they are and where they want to be |

## Linear Examples
None — this is a gap in Linear's current library. Diagnostic lead magnets represent an untapped opportunity, particularly:
- A **Creative Performance Scorecard** ("Rate your ad creative process across 8 dimensions")
- A **ROAS Readiness Quiz** ("Are you ready to scale? Take the 2-minute assessment")
- A **Creative Strategy Maturity Model** ("What stage is your creative operation at?")

Each of these would generate qualified leads while collecting data that informs the sales conversation.

## When NOT to Use
- **You don't have a clear next step based on the diagnosis.** If the result is "you scored 6/10" with no actionable follow-up, the prospect feels diagnosed but abandoned. Every result must connect to a recommendation — ideally, your offer.
- **The results can't be meaningfully personalized.** If everyone gets roughly the same output regardless of input, the diagnostic feels fake. The value is in the personalization; without it, you're just running a survey.
- **You lack the benchmarking data to make results credible.** "You're below average" only means something if you can show what average looks like. Without real data behind the scoring, the results feel arbitrary.
- **The diagnostic is too long or invasive.** Quizzes work because they're quick and fun. A 40-question audit with fields for revenue data will crater completion rates. Keep it under 10 questions for self-service formats.
