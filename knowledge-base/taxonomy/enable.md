# Enable

> **Verb:** Do | **Goal:** Reduce effort with tools

## Definition
Enable lead magnets give the prospect a functional tool that solves a real problem, making your brand indispensable through ongoing utility.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Consideration / Decision
- **Business types:** SaaS, tech companies, agencies with technical chops, data-driven brands
- **Audience types:** Action-takers, operators, and practitioners who value doing over reading
- **Goals:** High-intent lead capture, product-led growth, demonstrating capability, sticky engagement

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **ROI Calculators** | Input your numbers, see projected return | Medium | Your service/product has a quantifiable payoff |
| **Pricing Estimators** | Configure options, get a ballpark price | Medium | Your pricing is complex and prospects need clarity |
| **Savings Calculators** | Compare current cost vs. your solution | Medium | The value proposition is cost reduction |
| **Custom GPTs** | AI assistants trained on your methodology | Medium | You have a repeatable intellectual process that AI can mimic |
| **AI Chatbots** | Conversational tools that answer domain questions | High | Your audience needs on-demand, personalized answers |
| **AI Generators** | Tools that produce copy, images, strategies, etc. | High | The output is something your audience creates repeatedly |
| **Browser Extensions** | Lightweight tools that work inside the browser | High | The problem lives in a web-based workflow |
| **Plugins** | Add-ons for existing platforms (Figma, Shopify, etc.) | High | Your audience already lives in a specific tool ecosystem |
| **Spreadsheet Trackers** | Google Sheets / Excel tools that log and organize data | Low | The audience tracks something manually today |
| **Spreadsheet Dashboards** | Pre-built reporting views with charts and formulas | Medium | The audience needs visibility into metrics |
| **Budget Planners** | Financial planning spreadsheets with built-in logic | Medium | The audience allocates spend across categories |
| **Software Trials** | Time-limited access to your full product | High | You have a product that sells itself through usage |
| **Freemium Tools** | Permanently free tier with limited features | High | You want ongoing engagement and natural upsell |

## Linear Examples
- **Gross Profit Forecaster (Google Sheets)** — A spreadsheet tool that lets DTC brands project profitability. High utility, low friction (everyone has Google Sheets), and directly tied to the problem Linear solves.
- **ROAS Calculator & Bottleneck Finder (Google Sheets)** — Combines a calculator with diagnostic logic; the prospect inputs their numbers and the sheet highlights where performance is breaking down. Bridges the Enable and Diagnose categories.

## When NOT to Use
- **You lack the technical skills to build and maintain it.** A broken calculator or buggy GPT does more damage than no tool at all. Budget for QA and ongoing updates before committing.
- **The problem is better solved by education.** If the prospect doesn't understand the concept yet, handing them a tool is like giving a cockpit to someone who hasn't learned to fly. Educate first, then enable.
- **The tool would be too complex for self-service.** If the prospect needs a 20-minute walkthrough to use your spreadsheet, the tool isn't reducing effort — it's adding it. Simplify or repackage as a service.
- **You can't tie the tool back to your offer.** The best enable magnets create a natural bridge to your paid product. If the tool is useful but unrelated to what you sell, it attracts the wrong leads.
