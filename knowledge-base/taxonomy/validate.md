# Validate

> **Verb:** Justify decisions | **Goal:** Confidence through proof

## Definition
Validate lead magnets give the prospect evidence — real data, real results, real comparisons — so they can confidently justify a decision to themselves, their team, or their boss.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Consideration / Decision
- **Business types:** B2B companies, agencies, SaaS with measurable outcomes, any business where the buyer must justify the purchase to others
- **Audience types:** Decision-makers who need proof, committees that require documentation, analytical buyers who trust data over claims
- **Goals:** Building credibility, differentiating with original data, shortening sales cycles, arming champions with internal sell material

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Customer Case Studies** | Story-driven accounts of how a specific client achieved results | Medium | You have clients willing to be named and results worth showcasing |
| **Anonymized Success Stories** | Same structure as case studies, without naming the client | Medium | Results are strong but clients prefer privacy |
| **Survey Reports** | Original research from surveying your audience or industry | High | You can reach a statistically meaningful sample size |
| **Benchmark Reports** | Aggregated performance data across clients or the industry | High | You have access to enough data to establish meaningful baselines |
| **Trend Reports** | Forward-looking analysis based on data patterns | High | Your audience makes decisions based on where the market is heading |
| **Product Comparison Guides** | Side-by-side evaluations of competing solutions | Medium | Your audience is actively evaluating options and you can be credibly objective |
| **Vendor Evaluation Frameworks** | Criteria and scoring systems for choosing a provider | Medium | Your audience runs formal procurement processes |
| **Before/After Documentation** | Visual or numerical proof of transformation | Low | The change is dramatic and easy to understand at a glance |
| **ROI Outcome Summaries** | Documented financial impact of a specific engagement or product | Medium | Your audience needs to build a business case with hard numbers |

## Linear Examples
- **Our Ad Creative Benchmarks** — Benchmark report; gives DTC brands a data-backed reference point for what "good" looks like in ad creative performance. This is the most powerful type of validate magnet because it positions Linear as the source of truth for an entire category's metrics.

## When NOT to Use
- **You don't have real data or results to share.** Fabricated or exaggerated case studies backfire spectacularly. If you can't back up the numbers, don't publish them. One honest, modest case study beats ten inflated ones.
- **Your audience is at the awareness stage.** People who are still learning what the problem is aren't ready to compare solutions or review case studies. They need education first — validation comes later in the journey.
- **The data isn't compelling enough to differentiate you.** "We improved ROAS by 5%" doesn't stop the scroll. If your results are incremental, either wait until you have a stronger story or frame the data around a more interesting angle.
- **You're comparing yourself to competitors dishonestly.** Comparison guides that are transparently biased toward your own product destroy trust. If you can't be fair, stick to case studies where you only need to prove your own results.
