# Accelerate

> **Verb:** Start faster | **Goal:** Skip the blank page

## Definition
Accelerate lead magnets hand the prospect a pre-built starting point — templates, frameworks, and swipe files — so they can execute immediately instead of building from scratch.

## Best For
- **Buyer stage:** Decision
- **Business types:** Agencies, creative teams, operations-heavy companies, anyone who scales through repeatable processes
- **Audience types:** Practical, execution-oriented people who already understand the "why" and want the "how — right now"
- **Goals:** Attracting doers, demonstrating your methodology, shortening time-to-value, scaling operations

## Formats

| Format | Description | Effort | Best When |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Email Templates** | Pre-written emails for outreach, follow-ups, sequences | Low | Your audience sends repetitive emails |
| **Proposal Templates** | Structured docs for pitching services or projects | Medium | Your audience sells to clients |
| **SOP Templates** | Step-by-step process documents for teams | Medium | Your audience needs to systematize operations |
| **Script Templates** | Sales scripts, video scripts, call frameworks | Low | Your audience speaks to prospects or cameras |
| **Spreadsheet Templates** | Pre-formatted budgets, project plans, content calendars | Medium | The audience plans in spreadsheets |
| **Design Templates** | Canva, Figma, or social media post templates | Medium | Your audience creates visual content regularly |
| **Presentation Templates** | Slide decks for pitches, reports, or trainings | Medium | Your audience presents to stakeholders |
| **Notion / Airtable Templates** | Pre-built dashboards, wikis, CRMs, project boards | Medium | Your audience uses these tools and wants a head start |
| **Code Boilerplates** | Starter repos, config files, scaffold projects | High | Developer audience starting similar projects repeatedly |
| **Starter Kits** | Bundled code + docs + config for a specific use case | High | The setup cost of a project is a known pain point |
| **Decision Frameworks** | Structured models for making specific choices | Low | Your audience faces a recurring decision with multiple variables |
| **Mental Models** | Thinking tools that apply across situations | Low | You want to shape how your audience approaches problems |
| **Strategy Canvases** | One-page visual frameworks for planning | Medium | The audience needs to align a team around a plan |
| **Swipe Files** | Curated collections of proven examples to adapt | Low | Your audience creates similar assets and needs inspiration |

## When NOT to Use
- **Your audience doesn't know enough to use the template.** A copywriting framework is useless to someone who doesn't understand the basics of persuasion. If they need the "why" before the "how," start with Educate.
- **The template requires too much customization.** If the prospect has to rewrite 80% of your template, it's not accelerating anything — it's creating frustration. The best templates are 70%+ done.
- **The format doesn't match their workflow tools.** A Notion template for an audience that lives in Google Sheets is friction, not value. Match the delivery format to the tools your audience already uses.
- **You're templating something that shouldn't be templated.** Some work is inherently custom (brand strategy, product positioning). Forcing it into a template oversimplifies the problem and undermines your credibility.
