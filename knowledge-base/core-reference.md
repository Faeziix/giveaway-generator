## 1. The 6-Category Framework (Taxonomy)

| # | Category | Verb | Goal | What Prospect Gets |
|---|----------|------|------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Educate** | Learn | Build authority | Knowledge, understanding |
| 2 | **Enable** | Do | Reduce effort | A tool that does work for them |
| 3 | **Accelerate** | Start faster | Skip the blank page | A starting point to customize |
| 4 | **Diagnose** | Understand | Personalized insight | Clarity on where they stand |
| 5 | **Connect** | Access | Belonging, FOMO | Community, events, early access |
| 6 | **Validate** | Justify | Confidence through proof | Evidence, data, social proof |

### Formats by Category

**Educate:** Ebooks, guides, whitepapers, cheat sheets, video trainings, curated resource lists
**Enable:** Calculators, custom GPTs, browser extensions, spreadsheet tools, freemium trials
**Accelerate:** Templates (doc/spreadsheet/design/Notion/code), frameworks, SOPs, swipe files, checklists
**Diagnose:** Quizzes, scorecards/audits, self-assessments, benchmarking tools, diagnostic surveys
**Connect:** Private communities, gated content vaults, live events, beta access, newsletters
**Validate:** Case studies, research reports, comparison guides, ROI documentation

## 4. Design Principles (from 16 proven giveaways)

### Naming Principles
1. **Numbers as value signal** — Large specific numbers signal exhaustive coverage. "470+" beats "Many hooks."
2. **Format label required** — Always include format type (Calculator, Checklist, Templates, etc.) in the title.
3. **Proprietary framing** — "Our," company name, or "Internal" implies sharing real tools, not generic advice.
4. **One power word** — Bulletproof, Unlimited, High-Impact, Winning. Match to audience fear.
5. **Curated numbers** — Non-round numbers (9, 15, 25) feel more deliberate than 10, 20, 50.

### Format Selection Principles
6. **Interactive = Ownership** — When users input their data, the output becomes personally theirs. Calculators, fill-in templates > static PDFs.
7. **Recurring problem = Repeat usage** — Solve problems that return weekly/monthly. Swipe files, checklists, calculators have built-in retention.
8. **Compound value: Base + Bonus** — Add a second component (calculator + bottleneck finder, templates + AI prompts). Feels like two tools in one.
9. **SOP/Checklist = Professional rigor** — Operational formats appeal to teams and managers. Broader audience than individual-focused content.

### Content Structure Principles
10. **Exhaustive OR curated** — Go 100+ items (comprehensive) or 9-15 (best-of). Avoid 30-40 (neither).
11. **Specificity drives opt-ins** — Narrow topics convert better. "Q4 Creative Strategy" > "Marketing Tips."
12. **Seasonal urgency multiplies conversion** — Time-sensitive variants create natural urgency. Release 4-6 weeks before peak.
13. **Frictionless delivery** — Default to browser-based: Notion, Google Sheets, web pages. Avoid downloads or specialized software.

### Idea Quality Principles
14. **Artifact, not advice** — The lead magnet should feel like a stolen internal tool, not a blog post repackaged. "Our Top 9 Copywriting Frameworks" = artifact. "A Guide to Ad Copywriting" = advice. Test: "Would this sit on someone's desk or get bookmarked?" If it's a one-time read, turn it into a tool.
15. **Denominate value in outcomes** — Frame the lead magnet's value in results it produced, not effort to create. "The same 9 frameworks behind $500M+ in revenue" NOT "a 15-page copywriting document." The outcome IS the credibility.
16. **The "I should NOT be giving this away" test** — If the creator doesn't feel slightly uncomfortable sharing it, it's too generic. Push until it contains proprietary data, real processes, or hard-won experience that a competitor would pay for.

### Conversion Psychology Principles
17. **Solving urgent pain beats teaching theory** — Frame around the problem, not the knowledge. "Find Your Biggest Revenue Leak" > "Understanding Revenue Metrics."
18. **Low effort = High value perception** — Checklists and calculators feel expensive despite simple format. Optimize for perceived value gap.
19. **Team-ready formats broaden reach** — SOPs and checklists get shared internally. One download, multiple users.
20. **"Our" + Data = Maximum trust** — Proprietary data can't be Googled. Makes the email exchange feel fair.

## 5. Naming Conventions — Title Formulas

### Formula 1: Number + Power Word + Topic + Format
`[Number]+ [Power Word] [Industry/Topic] [Format Label]`
> 60+ Winning Video Ad Templates & AI Prompts

### Formula 2: Possessive + Format + Topic
`Our [Power Word] [Topic] [Format Label]`
> Our Top 9 Copywriting Frameworks

### Formula 3: Power Word + Topic + Format
`[Power Word] [Topic] [Format Label]`
> Unlimited Ad Variations Cheat Sheet

### Formula 4: How-to + Outcome
`How to [Action] [Desirable Outcome]`
> How to Build Your Own AI Creative Strategist

### Formula 5: Topic + Format + Benefit
`[Topic] [Format Label] [& Bonus Benefit]`
> ROAS Calculator & Bottleneck Finder

### Do's
- Lead with the most compelling element (number or power word)
- Be specific about the niche (not generic "marketing")
- Include format label always
- Use "&" to signal bonus value

### Don'ts
- Vague titles ("Marketing Guide")
- Stack multiple power words
- Forget the format label
- Use numbers for small collections (<5)
- Name it like a blog post ("The Complete Guide to...", "Everything You Need to Know...")
- Denominate value in pages, length, or creation effort

### The "Insider Artifact" Test (run on every title)
1. **Does it sound stolen from behind the curtain?** — "Our $2M+ Deal Negotiation Playbook" PASSES. "The Ultimate Guide to Negotiation" FAILS. Fix: add possessive framing + real results.
2. **Is value denominated in outcomes?** — "The Same 9 Frameworks Behind $500M+ in Revenue" PASSES. "9 Copywriting Frameworks (15 Pages)" FAILS. Fix: replace effort descriptors with credential numbers.
3. **Would a competitor pay for this?** — "Our Ad Creative Benchmarks" PASSES (proprietary data). "Ad Creative Best Practices" FAILS (freely available). Fix: push toward proprietary data, real processes, hard-won experience.

## 7. TOF Viral Post — LinkedIn Comment-Gate Formula

For maximum impressions at top-of-funnel, distribute lead magnets via LinkedIn comment-gated posts. Full playbook: `playbooks/tof-viral-post.md`.

### Post Anatomy (in order)
1. **Hook** — Bold claim with specific number/dollar amount OR contrarian statement (must work before "...see more" fold)
2. **Credential stack** — 2-4 proof points with specific numbers (revenue, volume, experience, client caliber)
3. **Problem agitation** — 3-5 arrow bullets (→) showing what the audience gets wrong or the pain you solved
4. **Bridge** — One key insight connecting problem to solution
5. **Resource intro** — Name the giveaway with proprietary framing ("our internal," "the exact system we use")
6. **"Inside you'll get" checklist** — 4-6 items with ✅ emojis, each independently valuable
7. **Social proof / scarcity line** — "I should NOT be giving this away" / restate the credential
8. **Comment-gate CTA** — Connect + comment one-word keyword (CAPS)
9. **Repost bonus** (optional) — 🚨 PS offering a second resource for resharing (~2x reach)

### 4 Post Formulas
| Formula | Structure | Best For | Comment Range |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **The Giveaway** | Hook → Credential → Problem → Resource → Checklist → CTA | Framework collections, swipe files | 100-537 |
| **The Breakdown** | Hook → List the items → Brief explanation each → CTA for full version | Numbered frameworks, lists | 200-400 |
| **The Story** | Hook → Problem story → Solution → Results → Resource → CTA | Tools, systems, SOPs | 400-761 |
| **The Contrarian** | Contrarian claim → Why they're wrong → Real insight → Resource → CTA | Challenging common practices | 200-350 |

### Best Resource Types for TOF Viral Posts
Framework collections, swipe files, cheat sheets, template libraries — LOW to MEDIUM effort, BROAD appeal. Broad > niche at TOF. "25 UGC Frameworks" > "UGC Frameworks for Skincare."

### Critical Rules
- **No links in the post** — LinkedIn suppresses posts with external URLs
- **Resource must be real** — deliver via DM, not a landing page
- **1-2 per month max** — avoid giveaway fatigue
- **Generous tone** — "100% free," "no email sign-up needed," "I should be charging for this"
- **Always include specific numbers** — vague claims get zero engagement
