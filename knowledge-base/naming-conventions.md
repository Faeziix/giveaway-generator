# Naming Conventions — Title Formulas & Patterns

Derived from analyzing Linear's 16 giveaways and broader lead magnet best practices.

## The 4 Core Patterns

### 1. Numbers Lead
Quantify value immediately. Bigger number = higher perceived value.

| Example | Number Used |
|---------|------------|
| **60+** Winning Video Ad Templates & AI Prompts | 60+ |
| **470+** High-Impact DTC Creative Hooks | 470+ |
| **25** High Performing UGC Frameworks | 25 |
| Our Top **9** Copywriting Frameworks | 9 |
| **15** Bulletproof Static Ad Concepts | 15 (in URL) |
| The **4** Core ROAS Metrics | 4 |

**Rule:** If your lead magnet contains countable items, lead with the number. Use "+" for collections over 50 to signal abundance.

### 2. Power Words
Convey insider knowledge, proven results, and exclusivity.

| Power Word | Used In |
|------------|---------|
| Winning | 60+ **Winning** Video Ad Templates |
| High-Impact | 470+ **High-Impact** DTC Creative Hooks |
| Bulletproof | **Bullet Proof** Static Ad Concepts |
| Unlimited | **Unlimited** Ad Variations Cheat Sheet |
| In-depth | **In-depth** Training / **In-depth** Whitelisting Cheat Sheet |
| High Performing | 25 **High Performing** UGC Frameworks |

**Rule:** Every title should have at least one power word that implies the content is tested, proven, or comprehensive.

### 3. Format Labels
Tell people exactly what they're getting. Reduces ambiguity, sets expectations.

| Label | Used In |
|-------|---------|
| Templates | Video Ad **Templates**, End Card **Templates** |
| Cheat Sheet | Whitelisting **Cheat Sheet**, Ad Variations **Cheat Sheet**, Q4 **Cheatsheet** |
| Frameworks | Copywriting **Frameworks**, UGC **Frameworks** |
| SOP | Customer Generated Content **SOP** |
| Checklist | Editing Review **Checklist** |
| Calculator | ROAS **Calculator** & Bottleneck Finder |
| Benchmarks | Ad Creative **Benchmarks** |
| Training | In-depth **Training** on the 4 Core ROAS Metrics |

**Rule:** Always include the format label. People decide whether to download based on the format as much as the topic.

### 4. Possessive Framing
"Our" signals insider access and proprietary knowledge.

| Example |
|---------|
| **Our** Top 9 Copywriting Frameworks |
| **Our** Ad Creative Benchmarks |
| **Our** In-depth Whitelisting Cheat Sheet |

**Rule:** Use "Our" when the content is based on proprietary data, internal processes, or hard-won experience. Don't overuse — save it for genuinely insider content.

## Title Formulas

### Formula 1: Number + Power Word + Topic + Format
Best for: Collections, swipe files, template packs
> **60+ Winning Video Ad Templates & AI Prompts**
> **470+ High-Impact DTC Creative Hooks**

Template: `[Number]+ [Power Word] [Industry/Topic] [Format Label]`

### Formula 2: Possessive + Format + Topic
Best for: Proprietary knowledge, internal processes
> **Our Top 9 Copywriting Frameworks**
> **Our Ad Creative Benchmarks**

Template: `Our [Power Word] [Topic] [Format Label]`

### Formula 3: Power Word + Topic + Format
Best for: Single-concept resources
> **Unlimited Ad Variations Cheat Sheet**
> **Bullet Proof Static Ad Concepts**

Template: `[Power Word] [Topic] [Format Label]`

### Formula 4: How-to + Outcome
Best for: Tutorials, trainings, guides
> **How to Build Your Own AI Creative Strategist**

Template: `How to [Action] [Desirable Outcome]`

### Formula 5: Topic + Format + Benefit
Best for: Tools that solve a specific problem
> **ROAS Calculator & Bottleneck Finder**
> **Gross Profit Forecaster**

Template: `[Topic] [Format Label] [& Bonus Benefit]`

## The "Insider Artifact" Naming Test

Before finalizing any title, run it through these 3 checks:

### 1. Does it sound like something stolen from behind the curtain?
- **Pass:** "Our $2M+ Deal Negotiation Playbook" — sounds like a real internal document
- **Fail:** "The Ultimate Guide to Real Estate Negotiation" — sounds like a blog post
- **Fix:** Add possessive framing ("Our," "The Exact," "My") and reference real results

### 2. Is the value denominated in outcomes, not effort?
- **Pass:** "The Same 9 Frameworks Behind $500M+ in Revenue" — value = revenue produced
- **Fail:** "9 Copywriting Frameworks (15 Pages)" — value = page count
- **Fix:** Replace effort descriptors with credential numbers. What has this tool/system actually produced?

### 3. Would a competitor pay money for this?
- **Pass:** "Our Ad Creative Benchmarks" — proprietary data competitors can't access
- **Fail:** "Ad Creative Best Practices" — freely available information repackaged
- **Fix:** Push toward proprietary data, real processes, or hard-won experience that can't be Googled

### Before/After Examples

| Weak (Advice) | Strong (Artifact) | What Changed |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Home Buying Guide | Our 27-Point Due Diligence Checklist for $2M+ Homes | Artifact framing, specificity, price anchor |
| Marketing Tips for Gyms | 200+ High-Converting Fitness Content Hooks | Number-led, power word, specific format |
| How to Price Your Services | The Pricing Calculator We Used to Hit $1M ARR | Outcome-denominated, possessive, tool not guide |
| Real Estate Market Report | Our $150M in Closed Luxury Sales, Exposed: Scottsdale Neighborhood Benchmarks | Credential, proprietary data, specific market |
| Social Media Content Ideas | 50+ Viral LinkedIn Post Templates & Our Exact Posting SOP | Number, compound value, operational format |

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Lead with the most compelling element (usually the number or power word)
- Be specific about the niche (DTC, UGC, Q4, ROAS — not generic "marketing")
- Include the format label so people know what they're downloading
- Use "&" to signal bonus value (Templates **&** AI Prompts, Calculator **&** Bottleneck Finder)
- Denominate value in outcomes/results, not pages or effort
- Frame as an internal artifact being shared, not advice being given

**Don't:**
- Use vague titles ("Marketing Guide", "Creative Resources")
- Stack too many power words ("Ultimate Winning Bulletproof Mega Templates")
- Forget the format — people want to know what they're getting
- Use numbers for small collections (don't say "3 Templates" — too low perceived value)
- Name it like a blog post or ebook ("The Complete Guide to...", "Everything You Need to Know About...")
- Denominate value in pages, length, or creation effort
