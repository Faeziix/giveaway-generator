## Target Pain Point
Brands know that customer-generated content (CGC) — real reviews, unboxings, testimonials from actual buyers — is gold for ad creative. But most have no systematic way to collect it. They get the occasional tagged post or review, but nothing repeatable. This SOP gives them a step-by-step operational process for sourcing CGC consistently.

## Content Structure
- Definition of CGC and how it differs from UGC and influencer content
- Step-by-step collection workflow (post-purchase email, SMS, incentive structure)
- Outreach templates for requesting content from customers
- Content brief or guidelines to send to participating customers
- Quality control criteria — what to accept, what to reshoot
- Filing and organization system for collected assets
- Legal/usage rights considerations (release language)

## What Makes It Effective
- **SOP format signals professional rigor** — this isn't a blog post with tips; it's a documented process. Appeals directly to operations managers, creative leads, and team builders.
- **"SOP" as a format label sets clear expectations** — the reader knows they're getting a step-by-step procedure they can hand to a team member and have executed.
- **Solves a specific operational gap** — plenty of content talks about why CGC matters, but very few resources provide the actual system for collecting it. This fills that void.
- **High implementation value** — the output of following this SOP is real content assets, making the ROI tangible and immediate.

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Accelerate** because it provides a ready-to-implement operational process — the user can take this SOP, hand it to their team, and start collecting content immediately. It's not primarily teaching theory (Educate) or providing benchmarks (Validate); it's giving them the machinery to execute. Could touch Educate if the reader needs to learn what CGC is first, but the core deliverable is the process itself.
