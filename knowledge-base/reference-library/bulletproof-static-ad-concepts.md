## Target Pain Point
Static ads get overshadowed by video in most creative strategy conversations, but they remain a critical part of any media mix — cheaper to produce, faster to iterate, and effective across placements. Brands need proven static ad concepts but struggle to find resources focused specifically on statics. These 15 concepts give them a ready-made inspiration library for high-performing image ads.

## Content Structure
- 15 static ad concepts, each with a name and visual description
- Layout or wireframe reference for each concept (composition, text placement, imagery style)
- Copy angle and messaging approach for each concept
- Use case guidance: which concepts work best for awareness vs. conversion vs. retargeting
- Variations and adaptation notes (how to customize for different products or offers)
- Examples of the concept in action or annotated breakdowns

## What Makes It Effective
- **"Bulletproof" is a strong power word** — implies these concepts are tested, reliable, and resistant to failure. Reduces the perceived risk of trying them.
- **"15" provides enough variety to be useful** — large enough to cover multiple angles and objectives, small enough to feel curated rather than overwhelming.
- **Fills a clear gap in the market** — the vast majority of ad creative resources focus on video and UGC. A resource dedicated to static ads stands out and serves an underserved audience.
- **Swipe file format has high repeat-use value** — creative teams return to swipe files again and again when brainstorming, making this a resource that stays open in browser tabs and bookmarks.

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Accelerate** because these are ready-to-reference concepts a creative team can use immediately in production — pick a concept, adapt it to the brand, and design. It's not teaching ad theory (Educate) or measuring performance (Validate); it's providing the creative starting points. Could touch Educate if it includes deep analysis of why each concept works, but the primary value is as a production-ready swipe file.
