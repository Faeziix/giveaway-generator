## Target Pain Point
End cards are the final frame of a video ad — the moment that drives the click, the swipe-up, the purchase. Yet most brands treat them as an afterthought, slapping on a logo and a generic "Shop Now." These templates ensure every video ad closes with a strong, conversion-optimized end card instead of a weak finish.

## Content Structure
- Collection of end card templates organized by style (minimal, bold, testimonial-backed, offer-focused, etc.)
- Visual layout guides or wireframes for each template
- Copy formulas for end card text (CTA, offer framing, urgency language)
- Best practices for end card design (text size, contrast, brand consistency)
- Guidance on matching end card style to ad type (UGC, studio, motion graphic)
- Notes on platform-specific considerations (aspect ratio, safe zones)

## What Makes It Effective
- **Hyper-specific niche topic** — by focusing exclusively on end cards rather than "ad templates" broadly, this avoids competition with the thousands of generic ad resources and feels surgically useful.
- **Templates = copy-paste ready** — the format promise is zero creative thinking required. Users can grab a template and apply it to their next video immediately.
- **Addresses a commonly overlooked element** — most creative strategy content focuses on hooks and opening frames. End cards are neglected, so this fills a real gap.
- **Small scope, outsized impact** — a better end card can meaningfully improve CTR across every video ad a brand runs, making this a high-leverage resource despite its narrow focus.

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Accelerate** because the templates are ready-to-use production assets. The user doesn't need to learn theory — they grab a template, customize it, and ship. Could touch Educate if it includes significant explanation of end card psychology, but the core deliverable is the templates themselves.
