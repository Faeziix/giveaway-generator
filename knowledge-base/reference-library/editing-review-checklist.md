## Target Pain Point
Video ad quality control is inconsistent, especially across teams. Editors miss details, creative directors catch errors too late, and there's no standardized process for reviewing ad creative before it goes live. This checklist ensures no detail is missed during post-production review, turning QC from a guessing game into a repeatable process.

## Content Structure
- Pre-review setup items (file naming, export settings, project organization)
- Visual quality checks (color grading, text legibility, brand consistency, safe zones)
- Audio quality checks (levels, music licensing, voiceover clarity, sound effects)
- Pacing and structure checks (hook timing, CTA placement, scene transitions, overall length)
- Compliance and platform checks (aspect ratios, platform specs, ad policy compliance)
- Final sign-off section with approval workflow

## What Makes It Effective
- **Checklists are the ultimate "save time" format** — no reading, no learning curve. Users open it, run through it, and immediately get value. The format itself signals efficiency.
- **Appeals to teams, not just individuals** — the QC process framing makes this relevant to creative directors, editors, and account managers alike. This broadens the audience beyond solo operators.
- **Operational focus implies professional-grade content** — a checklist for editing review signals that Linear operates at a level of rigor that most agencies and brands aspire to. It's aspirational and practical simultaneously.
- **"Linear Editing Review Checklist" uses branded naming** — putting the company name in the title positions this as Linear's actual internal process being shared, which adds authenticity and insider appeal.

## Industry Adaptations
- **B2B SaaS:** "Product Launch QA Checklist" — replace video editing checks with launch readiness items (landing page, email sequences, docs, billing integration, monitoring), same team-oriented sign-off workflow
- **E-commerce:** "Product Listing Quality Checklist" — replace editing checks with listing completeness (photos, descriptions, SEO, pricing, inventory), compliance checks become marketplace policy compliance
- **Agency:** "Client Deliverable QA Checklist" — replace video-specific checks with deliverable-type checks (design, copy, strategy docs), keep the branded naming pattern and multi-role approval workflow
- **Local Services:** "Customer Experience Audit Checklist: 20 Touchpoints That Drive 5-Star Reviews" — replace creative QC with customer journey touchpoints (first call, estimate, job completion, follow-up)

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Accelerate** because checklists are process shortcuts — they codify expertise into a scannable, repeatable format that speeds up execution. Users aren't learning why each check matters (Educate) or building a custom system (Enable); they're adopting a ready-made QC process to immediately improve their workflow. Could touch Enable if the checklist is framed as operational infrastructure, but the primary value is acceleration of the review process.
