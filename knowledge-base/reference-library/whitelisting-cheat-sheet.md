## Target Pain Point
Influencer whitelisting (running paid ads through a creator's account) is one of the most effective tactics in performance marketing, but the setup process is confusing. Brands need to navigate Business Manager permissions, creator access levels, and platform-specific quirks. This cheat sheet condenses the entire process into a scannable reference.

## Content Structure
- Definition of whitelisting and why it matters
- Step-by-step setup instructions for Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- Permission levels explained (what to request from creators)
- Common troubleshooting issues and fixes
- Best practices for managing whitelisted ads at scale
- Quick-reference checklist for onboarding new creators

## What Makes It Effective
- **"In-depth" + "Cheat Sheet" is a compelling tension** — promises thoroughness in a compact, scannable format. Users feel they're getting depth without the time commitment.
- **"Our" signals proprietary knowledge** — implies this is the exact process Linear uses internally, not generic advice repackaged from help docs.
- **Niche topic with less competition** — whitelisting is specific enough that there aren't many free resources covering it well, making this stand out in search and social.
- **Solves an immediate, practical problem** — someone searching for whitelisting help has a task to complete right now, making the lead magnet conversion natural.

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Educate** because its primary function is teaching a process the reader doesn't yet understand. It's instructional — explaining what whitelisting is, how it works, and how to set it up. Could touch Accelerate if it includes copy-paste templates for creator outreach, but the core value is knowledge transfer.
