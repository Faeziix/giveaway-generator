## Target Pain Point
Most brands use UGC but treat it as unstructured — they brief a creator, hope for the best, and get inconsistent results. They lack repeatable structural patterns for UGC that actually converts. These 25 frameworks give them proven, reusable blueprints so every piece of UGC follows a high-performing structure.

## Content Structure
- 25 distinct UGC frameworks, each with a name, structure breakdown, and use case
- Framework anatomy: hook type, body structure, CTA pattern
- Examples or descriptions of when to use each framework (e.g., problem-solution, unboxing, routine integration, before/after)
- Categorization by ad objective or funnel stage (awareness, consideration, conversion)
- Guidance on matching frameworks to product types or verticals
- Tips for briefing creators using these frameworks

## What Makes It Effective
- **"25" is a strong number** — large enough to feel comprehensive and provide real variety, but not so large that it overwhelms. Signals depth without bloat.
- **"High Performing" implies data-backed selection** — these aren't theoretical ideas; the framing suggests they've been tested and proven in real campaigns.
- **Frameworks are inherently reusable** — unlike a one-time tip, a framework can be applied across dozens of campaigns, increasing the perceived long-term ROI of downloading.
- **Fills a genuine gap in available resources** — most UGC advice is vague ("be authentic"). Structured frameworks with names and patterns are rare and highly valued by creative teams.

## Taxonomy Mapping
Falls under **Accelerate** because the frameworks are ready-to-use production tools — a creative team can take any of the 25 and immediately brief a creator against it. The value is in the shortcut to execution, not in teaching UGC theory. Could touch Educate if it includes significant context on why each framework works, but the primary utility is as a working reference library for content production.
