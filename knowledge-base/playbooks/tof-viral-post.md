# TOF Viral Post — LinkedIn Comment-Gate Playbook

## What It Is

A LinkedIn post designed to maximize impressions and engagement by giving away a free resource in exchange for a comment + connection. This is NOT an email opt-in lead magnet — it's a top-of-funnel awareness play where the goal is pure reach. The resource itself can be any low-to-medium effort format (cheat sheet, framework collection, template library, etc.), but the POST is the product.

The post follows a proven viral structure that triggers LinkedIn's algorithm by generating high comment volume, which pushes the post to 10-100x the creator's follower count.

---

## When to Use It

- **Buyer stage:** Awareness (people who don't know you yet)
- **Goal:** Maximize impressions, grow network, build authority
- **Best for:** Any industry where the audience is active on LinkedIn
- **Taxonomy fit:** The post is the distribution layer — the giveaway inside can be any category (Educate, Accelerate, Enable, etc.)
- **NOT for:** Qualified lead gen, bottom-of-funnel conversion, email list building (use gated landing pages for those)

## Anatomy of a Viral TOF Post

Every high-performing post (225-761+ comments) follows this structure in order:

### 1. The Hook (Lines 1-2)
The first 1-2 lines must stop the scroll. They appear before the "...see more" truncation. Three proven hook types:

**A. Bold Claim + Specificity**
> "This AI system generates 87% of our winning ads."
> "We've used the same 9 copywriting frameworks to generate $500M+ for our clients."

**B. Contrarian/Pattern Interrupt**
> "If you're outsourcing all your copywriting to ChatGPT, you're making a huge mistake."
> "Most creative strategists obsess over hooks. Very few obsess over CTAs."

**C. Generous Giveaway Declaration**
> "I'm giving my most valuable internal resource away."
> "The best performing Meta ads don't look like ads."

**Rules:**
- Must create curiosity or tension that demands the click
- Include a specific number, dollar amount, or surprising claim
- Never start with "I'm excited to share..." or "I've been thinking about..."

### 2. Credential Stack (Lines 3-8)
Establish WHY anyone should care. Stack 2-4 proof points using specific numbers:

- Revenue generated: "$500M+ in trackable revenue"
- Volume produced: "10,000+ UGC creatives," "2000+ ads monthly"
- Time invested: "5 years," "last 2 years perfecting this"
- Client caliber: "7 and 8-figure DTC brands," "8-figure brands"

**Rules:**
- Use specific, non-round numbers when possible (87% > ~90%)
- Stack credentials, don't just state one
- Credentials must connect to the resource being offered

### 3. Problem Agitation (Lines 9-20)
Show the pain the audience is feeling. Use arrow bullets (→) for visual rhythm:

> → No system
> → No frameworks
> → No data

Or tell a brief story of a problem you faced:

> → Clients were scaling fast & demanding more creative
> → We had a growing waitlist of brands ready to pay
> → Our team was maxed out with zero capacity

**Rules:**
- 3-5 arrow bullets maximum
- Each bullet is a short, punchy phrase (under 10 words ideal)
- The pain must be something the reader recognizes in themselves
- Can be framed as "what most people get wrong" OR "the problem we solved"

### 4. The Bridge (1-3 lines)
Transition from pain to solution. Explain the key insight or why this resource exists:

> "Every winning ad we produce starts with one of these 9 frameworks."
> "The best use of AI is giving your best people leverage to do more of what they're best at."
> "A strong, well-placed end card can 2-3x your CTR on the same creative."

**Rules:**
- One clear insight, not three
- Should make the reader nod or feel "that's exactly right"

### 5. The Resource Introduction (1-3 lines)
Name what you're giving away and frame it as internal/proprietary:

> "I'm giving away our Master DTC Copywriting Bundle that we use internally..."
> "I'm giving away our internal Whitelisting Cheatsheet that we use with every client — completely free."
> "I just packaged all 25 frameworks into a single resource we use internally at Linear."

**Key phrases to use:**
- "I'm giving away..."
- "our internal [resource]"
- "we use with every client"
- "completely free"
- "the exact [system/process/framework] we [use/follow]"
- "I should be charging a lot of money for this"

### 6. The "Inside You'll Get" Checklist (4-6 items)
Use checkmark emojis (✅) to list what's inside. Each item should feel like a standalone valuable thing:

> ✅ 9 proven copywriting frameworks for Meta ads
> ✅ Breakdown and examples for each framework
> ✅ Our exact ChatGPT prompts for ad ideation and scriptwriting
> ✅ And a lot more

**Rules:**
- 4-6 items (not 3, not 8)
- Start each with a number or specific descriptor
- At least one item should reference a trending topic (AI prompts, automation, etc.)
- Last item can be "And a lot more" or the most impressive item
- Alternate between breadth items ("25+ frameworks") and depth items ("real examples from 8-figure brands")

### 7. Social Proof / Scarcity Line (1-2 lines)
Add one line that creates urgency or reinforces value:

> "These are the same frameworks behind $200M+ in trackable revenue for our clients."
> "One of my clients told me I should NOT be giving this away."
> "I should be charging a lot of money for this — but today you can have it for 100% free."
> "We use this resource every single day to ensure our creatives consistently hit 1%+ CTR."

### 8. The Comment-Gate CTA (3-4 lines)
Always the same structure:

> To get access, all you need to do is:
>
> 1. Connect with me [Name]
> 2. Comment '[KEYWORD]' below
>
> And I'll send [resource name] straight over.

**Keyword rules:**
- One word, ALL CAPS or bold Unicode
- Related to the topic: COPY, UGC, AI, WHITELIST, CARD
- Short and memorable

### 9. The Repost Bonus (optional but high-impact)
A PS section offering a bonus resource for reposting. Uses alarm emoji (🚨) for attention:

> 🚨 PS. If you repost this, I'll also send over [bonus resource] 🚨

**Rules:**
- Bonus must be genuinely different from main resource
- Frame it as complementary: "Combine this with [main resource] and..."
- Use urgency framing: "when you repost" not "if you repost"
- This section alone can 2-3x the post's reach

## The 4 Proven Post Formulas

### Formula A: "The Giveaway" (Highest comment volume)
Hook → Credential → Problem → Resource intro → Inside checklist → Scarcity → CTA → Repost bonus
**Best for:** Framework collections, swipe files, template libraries
**Examples:** Copywriting Bundle (101 comments), UGC Frameworks (537 comments)

### Formula B: "The Breakdown + Giveaway" (Authority + engagement)
Hook → List/breakdown of the core concepts → Brief explanation of each → CTA to get the full version
**Best for:** Framework collections where listing the frameworks IS compelling content
**Examples:** 9 Copywriting Frameworks list post (348 comments)

### Formula C: "The Story + Giveaway" (Highest quality engagement)
Hook → Problem story → What you tried → The solution → Results → Resource intro → CTA
**Best for:** Tools, systems, SOPs where the backstory adds credibility
**Examples:** AI Creative Strategist (761 comments)

### Formula D: "The Contrarian + Giveaway" (Strongest hook)
Contrarian claim → Why most people are wrong → The real insight → Resource that solves it → CTA
**Best for:** Topics where conventional wisdom is wrong
**Examples:** "Most strategists obsess over hooks" (283 comments), "Best Meta ads don't look like ads" (266 comments)

## Step-by-Step Creation Process

### Step 1: Choose the Resource
Pick a low-to-medium effort resource that has broad appeal:
- **Best performers:** Framework collections, swipe files, cheat sheets, template libraries
- **The resource must be REAL** — something you actually use internally. If it doesn't exist yet, build it first.
- **Broad > Niche for TOF** — "25 UGC Frameworks" reaches more people than "UGC Frameworks for Supplement Brands"

### Step 2: Identify the Credential Stack
What proof do you have that makes this resource credible?
- Revenue numbers (trackable results)
- Volume numbers (ads produced, clients served, years of experience)
- Client caliber (brand sizes, recognizable names)
- Specific results (CPA reduction, CTR improvement, output increase)

### Step 3: Write the Hook
Use one of the three hook types. Test by asking: "Would I stop scrolling for this?" The hook must work WITHOUT any context — it appears alone before the fold.

### Step 4: Write the Problem Agitation
Choose one:
- **"Most people" framing:** Show what the audience does wrong (3-5 arrow bullets)
- **Story framing:** Tell the specific problem you/your team faced
- **Contrarian framing:** Challenge a common belief

### Step 5: Write the "Inside" Checklist
List 4-6 specific items inside the resource. Each should feel independently valuable. Include at least one item that references a current trend (AI, automation, etc.).

### Step 6: Write the CTA + Bonus
- Pick a one-word keyword related to the topic
- Write the comment-gate CTA
- Optional: add a repost bonus with a complementary resource

### Step 7: Format Check
Before posting, verify:
- [ ] Hook works in isolation (before "...see more")
- [ ] At least 2 specific credential numbers
- [ ] Arrow bullets (→) for problem section
- [ ] Checkmark emojis (✅) for "inside" section
- [ ] One-word comment keyword in caps
- [ ] No links in the post (LinkedIn suppresses posts with links)
- [ ] Post length: 150-250 words (long enough for value, short enough to read)
- [ ] Generous tone throughout ("I'm giving away," "100% free," "no email sign-up needed")

## Naming the Resource (for the post)

The resource name inside the post follows different rules than a landing page title. It should feel internal and premium:

**Formula:** [Possessive] + [Descriptor] + [Topic] + [Format]
- "Our Master DTC Copywriting Bundle"
- "Our internal Whitelisting Cheatsheet"
- "The full End Card Template Library"
- "Our database of 470+ high-performance hooks"

**Rules:**
- Always include "our" or "internal" or "the exact"
- Add "Master," "Full," or "Complete" as a size signal
- Include the format word (Bundle, Cheatsheet, Library, Database, System)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Making the resource too niche** — TOF is about breadth. "25 UGC Frameworks" > "UGC Frameworks for Skincare Brands"
2. **Weak credentials** — If you can't stack at least 2 impressive numbers, the post won't convert
3. **Including links** — LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses posts with external links. Put the link in DMs only
4. **Generic hook** — "I created a free resource" gets zero engagement. The hook needs tension or specificity
5. **Too many items in the checklist** — 4-6 items. More than that and the post feels like a wall of text
6. **Forgetting the repost bonus** — This is free extra reach. Always include it if you have a second resource
7. **Resource doesn't actually exist** — People talk. If the resource is garbage, it kills trust permanently
