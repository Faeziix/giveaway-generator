# Benchmark Report Playbook

## What It Is
A benchmark report is a data-driven document that establishes performance standards for a specific industry, channel, or activity by aggregating and analyzing real data. It answers the question every professional quietly asks: "How do I compare?" Benchmark reports carry outsized authority because they require proprietary data that the audience cannot generate on their own, making them one of the most powerful trust-building lead magnets.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Top-of-funnel (awareness) and mid-funnel (consideration)
- **Best industries:** Agencies (with client data), SaaS platforms (with usage data), e-commerce aggregators, any business that sits on meaningful performance data
- **Taxonomy categories:** Validate
- **Use when:** You have access to proprietary or aggregated data that your audience cannot get elsewhere, you want to establish category authority and earn media/PR coverage, or your audience makes decisions based on comparative performance (e.g., "Is my CTR good?")

## Anatomy of a Good Benchmark Report
- **Executive summary:** 1-page overview of the most important findings. Busy readers should get the core insights here without reading further.
- **Methodology:** How the data was collected, sample size, time period, and any caveats. Transparency builds trust. Do not skip this section.
- **Key findings with data visualizations:** 5-10 headline findings, each supported by a chart, graph, or data table. Each finding should include a plain-language interpretation and a recommended action.
- **Industry breakdowns:** Performance data segmented by industry, company size, channel, or other relevant dimensions. This is where personalization happens -- readers find their segment and compare.
- **Recommendations:** Based on the data, what should readers do? This section bridges the gap between "interesting data" and "actionable insight."
- **Appendix with full data:** Complete data tables for readers who want to dig deeper. This adds credibility and serves as a reference resource.
- **Design:** Professional, data-forward layout. Consistent chart styling. Branded but not salesy. This should feel like a research publication, not a marketing brochure.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Define the scope** -- What specific area will you benchmark? Narrow scope produces more useful reports. "Ad creative benchmarks" is better than "digital marketing benchmarks." Define the metrics, time period, and segments you will cover.
2. **Gather and clean the data** -- Pull data from your platform, client accounts (anonymized), or surveys. Clean it thoroughly -- outliers, missing data, and inconsistencies will undermine credibility. Document your methodology as you go.
3. **Analyze and find the story** -- Run the numbers and identify the 5-10 most interesting, surprising, or actionable findings. Look for patterns across segments, changes over time, and counterintuitive results. The story is what makes the data memorable.
4. **Visualize the data** -- Create clear, honest charts and graphs for each key finding. Avoid chartjunk. Every visualization should have a clear takeaway that the reader can grasp in 5 seconds. Label axes, include sample sizes, and add context.
5. **Write the narrative** -- For each finding, write a plain-language explanation: what the data shows, why it matters, and what the reader should do about it. The narrative turns data into insight.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. Include the year or time period to signal freshness.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Design as a professional PDF report. Consider also building a web version with interactive charts. Gate the full report behind email capture, but share 2-3 key findings publicly for PR and social sharing.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "[Year] [Topic] Benchmarks" -- e.g., *2025 Ad Creative Benchmarks*
- **Formula 2:** "The [Industry] [Topic] Report: [Year] Edition" -- e.g., *The DTC Performance Creative Report: Q4 2025 Edition*
- **Formula 3:** "[Topic] Benchmarks: [Number] [Metric] Across [Number] [Segment]" -- e.g., *Creative Performance Benchmarks: 12 Metrics Across 8 Industries*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Linear Examples
- **Our Ad Creative Benchmarks** -- Works because "our" signals proprietary data (not publicly available), "ad creative" is precisely scoped, and benchmarks are inherently high-value for anyone running ads who wants to know if their numbers are good.

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 1-3 weeks (data collection, analysis, visualization, writing, and design)
- **Skills needed:** Data analysis (statistics, spreadsheet/SQL skills), writing (translating data into narrative), design (data visualization and report layout)
- **Maintenance:** Periodic updates -- annual or quarterly releases. Each new edition is a content event that drives fresh leads. Plan the update cadence before publishing the first edition.
