# Custom GPT Playbook

## What It Is
A Custom GPT is a purpose-built AI assistant configured with a specific system prompt, knowledge base, and behavior rules to perform a narrow task exceptionally well. Instead of handing someone a static document, you give them a living tool that responds to their specific inputs with tailored outputs. It is the newest lead magnet format and carries a strong novelty factor -- most audiences have never received an AI tool as a giveaway.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Mid-funnel (consideration) and bottom-funnel (decision)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, SaaS, consultancies, coaches, any business with a repeatable methodology that can be encoded into prompts
- **Taxonomy categories:** Enable
- **Use when:** You have a repeatable process or decision framework that can be expressed as a prompt, your audience would benefit from on-demand personalized outputs (e.g., generating copy, analyzing data, diagnosing problems), or you want to showcase your methodology in an interactive format that feels high-value

## Anatomy of a Good Custom GPT
- **Clear purpose/use case:** The GPT does one thing well. "Creative brief generator" is a Custom GPT. "Marketing helper" is not. Narrow scope produces better outputs and clearer positioning.
- **System prompt:** The core instructions that define the GPT's personality, expertise, methodology, constraints, and output format. This is where your intellectual property lives.
- **Knowledge base/files:** Uploaded documents (frameworks, SOPs, examples, data) that the GPT references when generating responses. This is what makes your GPT different from a generic ChatGPT conversation.
- **Example conversations:** 3-5 sample interactions that demonstrate the GPT's capability and show users what to ask. These serve as onboarding and set expectations.
- **Limitations noted:** Transparent communication about what the GPT can and cannot do. This prevents frustration and builds trust.
- **Iteration based on user feedback:** A plan for collecting feedback and improving the GPT over time. The first version is never the final version.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Define the single use case** -- What specific task will this GPT perform? Write it as a sentence: "This GPT helps [audience] do [task] by [method]." If you cannot state the use case in one sentence, the scope is too broad.
2. **Write the system prompt** -- This is the most important step. Define the GPT's role, expertise, methodology, output format, tone, and constraints. Be specific. Include step-by-step instructions for how the GPT should process user inputs. Test and refine until the outputs are consistently high quality.
3. **Prepare the knowledge base** -- Gather the documents the GPT needs to reference: your frameworks, templates, SOPs, example outputs, data sets, or style guides. Upload them as files. The knowledge base is what makes outputs specific to your methodology rather than generic.
4. **Build example conversations** -- Create 3-5 starter prompts that showcase the GPT's best capabilities. These should demonstrate different use cases and input types. Users who see good examples produce better inputs.
5. **Test extensively** -- Have 5-10 people outside your team use the GPT and provide feedback. Test edge cases, bad inputs, and off-topic requests. Refine the system prompt based on where the GPT fails or produces mediocre outputs.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. The name should describe the function, not the technology.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Share as a GPT link (if using OpenAI's platform) or embed in your own interface. Include a brief onboarding guide explaining what the GPT does, how to use it, and what to expect. Gate access behind email capture.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "[Function] AI" -- e.g., *Creative Brief Generator AI*
- **Formula 2:** "The AI [Role]" -- e.g., *The AI Creative Strategist*
- **Formula 3:** "[Your Brand] [Function] Assistant" -- e.g., *Linear Ad Copy Assistant*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 4-12 hours (system prompt engineering, knowledge base preparation, testing, and refinement)
- **Skills needed:** Prompt engineering (writing effective system prompts), testing (systematic evaluation of outputs), domain expertise (the methodology encoded in the GPT)
- **Maintenance:** Periodic updates as your methodology evolves, user feedback reveals gaps, or the underlying AI platform changes capabilities. Plan to review and refine monthly for the first quarter after launch.
