# Quiz / Assessment Playbook

## What It Is
A quiz or assessment is an interactive experience where the user answers a series of questions and receives a personalized result, score, or diagnosis. It combines engagement (people love learning about themselves) with segmentation (you learn about them based on their answers). Quizzes are among the highest-converting lead magnet formats because the result creates a natural reason to exchange an email.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Top-of-funnel (awareness) and mid-funnel (consideration)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, coaches, SaaS, e-commerce, any business that serves distinct audience segments
- **Taxonomy categories:** Diagnose
- **Use when:** Your audience has a problem but does not know how severe it is (or what type they are), you want to segment leads based on their answers for personalized follow-up, or you want a format with high viral/share potential

## Anatomy of a Good Quiz / Assessment
- **7-15 questions:** Enough to feel meaningful, not so many that people abandon. Each question should map to a scoring axis.
- **Scoring system:** A clear, defensible logic that maps answers to results. Can be points-based, category-based, or branching.
- **3-5 result categories:** Each result should feel distinct and accurate. Avoid results that feel negative -- frame every outcome as an opportunity.
- **Personalized results page:** The result name, a description of what it means, specific strengths, specific areas to improve, and a recommended next step.
- **CTA based on result:** The call-to-action should be tailored to the result. Someone who scores "beginner" gets a different offer than someone who scores "advanced."
- **Optional email capture for detailed results:** Show a teaser of the result for free, but gate the full breakdown (detailed analysis, personalized recommendations, comparison to peers) behind an email.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Define the diagnostic question** -- What is the one question your audience wants answered about themselves? Frame the quiz around a single axis: "What type of X are you?" or "How good is your Y?" or "Where is your biggest Z gap?"
2. **Design the result categories** -- Start with the outputs, not the questions. Define 3-5 distinct result types, each with a name, description, and recommended action. Make sure every result feels valuable (no one wants to be told they are bad at something with no path forward).
3. **Write the questions** -- Each question should help distinguish between result categories. Avoid yes/no questions -- use multiple choice with 3-5 options. Keep questions conversational and jargon-free.
4. **Build the scoring logic** -- Map each answer option to one or more result categories. Test with real scenarios to make sure the scoring produces accurate, useful results. Have 5-10 people take the quiz and validate that their results feel right.
5. **Design the results experience** -- Create a dedicated results page for each category. Include the result name, what it means, specific recommendations, and a CTA. This is where the real value lives.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. The name should promise self-knowledge or diagnosis.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Use a quiz platform (Typeform, Interact, ScoreApp, or custom-built). Set up email capture between completion and results. Build automated follow-up sequences segmented by result.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "What's Your [Topic] Type?" -- e.g., *What's Your Ad Creative Strategy Type?*
- **Formula 2:** "The [Topic] Assessment: Find Your [Metric/Gap]" -- e.g., *The Creative Performance Assessment: Find Your Biggest Growth Gap*
- **Formula 3:** "How [Adjective] Is Your [Topic]? Take the Quiz" -- e.g., *How Effective Is Your Ad Creative? Take the 2-Minute Quiz*

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 8-16 hours (includes question writing, scoring logic, results pages, and platform setup)
- **Skills needed:** Writing (conversational question crafting), quiz logic (scoring systems), design (results pages and platform configuration)
- **Maintenance:** One-time initial build, with periodic refinement based on completion rates, result distribution, and user feedback
