# Checklist Playbook

## What It Is
A checklist is a structured list of action items or verification points that guides someone through a process from start to finish. It turns complex workflows into a simple, sequential set of tasks that can be checked off one by one, reducing errors and ensuring nothing gets missed.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Top-of-funnel (awareness) and mid-funnel (consideration)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, SaaS, e-commerce, content creators, operations-heavy businesses
- **Taxonomy categories:** Accelerate
- **Use when:** Your audience needs to execute a repeatable process correctly, you want to provide a quick win that builds trust, or you want to simplify something that feels overwhelming into manageable steps

## Anatomy of a Good Checklist
- **Title:** Specific, outcome-oriented (what they will achieve by completing it)
- **Intro sentence:** 1-2 sentences explaining who the checklist is for and when to use it
- **Numbered items (10-25):** Each item has a checkbox and a brief explanation (1-2 sentences) of why it matters or how to do it
- **Logical grouping:** Items grouped into phases or sections if the process has distinct stages
- **Summary/next step:** A closing section that tells them what to do after completing the checklist
- **Design:** Clean layout, generous whitespace, printable format (PDF works well)
- **Length:** 1-3 pages maximum

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Identify the process** -- Pick one specific workflow your audience struggles with. The more specific, the more useful (e.g., "post-production editing review" beats "video editing tips").
2. **Brain-dump every step** -- Write out every single action item without editing. Include things that seem obvious -- people skip obvious steps all the time.
3. **Order and group** -- Arrange items in the sequence they should be completed. Group related items under section headers if the list exceeds 12 items.
4. **Write brief explanations** -- Add 1-2 sentences per item explaining the why or the how. This is what separates a useful checklist from a generic to-do list.
5. **Cut ruthlessly** -- Remove anything that is not actionable. Every item should start with a verb. Aim for 10-25 items total.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. Use a title that promises a clear outcome.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Format as a clean PDF with checkboxes. Consider also offering a Google Doc or Notion version for digital use.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "The [Specific Process] Checklist" -- e.g., *The Pre-Launch Ad Creative Checklist*
- **Formula 2:** "[Number]-Point [Outcome] Checklist" -- e.g., *15-Point Video Ad QC Checklist*
- **Formula 3:** "The Complete [Task] Review Checklist" -- e.g., *The Complete Post-Production Editing Review Checklist*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Linear Examples
- **Post Production Editing Review Checklist** -- Works because it targets a specific, repeatable moment in the creative workflow where mistakes are costly. The specificity of "post-production editing review" signals deep expertise.

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 1-2 hours
- **Skills needed:** Writing (subject-matter expertise in the process being documented)
- **Maintenance:** One-time (update only if the underlying process changes)
