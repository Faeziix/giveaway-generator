# Video Training Playbook

## What It Is
A video training is a recorded educational session -- or series of sessions -- that teaches a specific skill or concept through visual demonstration. Video combines the depth of a guide with the personality and clarity of a live presentation. It is the highest-trust format because the audience sees and hears a real person, which accelerates relationship-building in a way static documents cannot.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Mid-funnel (consideration) and bottom-funnel (decision)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, coaches, SaaS, e-commerce, education, any business where the founder or team has strong on-camera presence
- **Taxonomy categories:** Educate
- **Use when:** The topic is best understood through visual demonstration (screen walkthroughs, before/after comparisons), you want to build personal connection and trust at scale, or you are targeting an audience that prefers watching over reading

## Anatomy of a Good Video Training
- **Hook/intro (30-60 seconds):** State the problem, promise the outcome, and explain why the viewer should keep watching. Establish credibility quickly.
- **Agenda (15-30 seconds):** Brief overview of what will be covered. Sets expectations and reduces drop-off.
- **3-5 core teaching segments:** Each segment covers one concept or skill. Each should be self-contained enough that someone could skip to it and still get value. Include transitions between segments.
- **Examples/demos:** Live walkthroughs, screen shares, case studies, or before/after comparisons that show the concept in action. Telling is not enough -- showing is what makes video training valuable.
- **Recap (1-2 minutes):** Summarize the key takeaways. Repeat the most important points.
- **CTA:** A clear next step -- book a call, download a companion resource, watch the next video, or join a program.
- **Production quality:** Clean audio is non-negotiable. Video quality should be professional but does not need to be cinematic. Good lighting and a clean background are sufficient.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Script the outline** -- Write a detailed outline (not a word-for-word script) covering the hook, each teaching segment, transitions, and the CTA. Time each section. Aim for 15-45 minutes total for a single training. Longer than 45 minutes and you should consider splitting into a series.
2. **Prepare visual assets** -- Create any slides, screen recordings, or examples you will reference. Have these ready before recording so you can move through them smoothly.
3. **Record** -- Use a quality microphone and good lighting. Record in one pass if possible (editing is expensive). If doing a screen walkthrough, practice the workflow twice before hitting record. Speak conversationally, not like you are reading.
4. **Edit** -- Cut dead air, false starts, and tangents. Add lower-thirds or text overlays for key points. Keep the pacing tight -- every minute should deliver value.
5. **Create companion materials** -- Build a summary document, worksheet, or resource list that accompanies the video. This gives the viewer something to reference after watching and increases perceived value.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. The title should promise a specific skill or insight.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Host on a platform that supports email gating (Wistia, Vimeo with privacy settings, or a landing page with embedded video). Include the companion materials as a downloadable bonus. Consider offering a transcript for accessibility.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "[Topic] Training: [Specific Outcome]" -- e.g., *ROAS Metrics Training: The 4 Numbers That Actually Matter*
- **Formula 2:** "How to [Achieve Outcome]: [Duration] Video Training" -- e.g., *How to Diagnose Ad Fatigue: 20-Minute Video Training*
- **Formula 3:** "The [Adjective] [Topic] Masterclass" -- e.g., *The Complete Creative Testing Masterclass*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Linear Examples
- **In-depth Training on 4 Core ROAS Metrics** -- Works because it narrows focus to exactly 4 metrics (specific and bounded), uses "in-depth" to signal real substance, and targets a universal pain point (understanding ROAS beyond the surface).

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 1-4 weeks (scripting, recording, editing, companion materials)
- **Skills needed:** Video production (recording setup, lighting, audio), scripting (outlining and presenting), editing (video editing software)
- **Maintenance:** One-time. Videos have a long shelf life as long as the content remains accurate. Update only if tools, platforms, or strategies covered in the training change significantly.
