# Guide / Ebook Playbook

## What It Is
A guide or ebook is a long-form educational document that provides comprehensive coverage of a topic from start to finish. It is the most traditional lead magnet format -- a structured, multi-chapter resource that builds understanding progressively. When done well, a guide positions you as the definitive authority on a subject. When done poorly, it becomes a PDF that sits unread in someone's downloads folder.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Mid-funnel (consideration) and bottom-funnel (decision)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, SaaS, B2B services, consultancies, any business where expertise and trust are key buying factors
- **Taxonomy categories:** Educate
- **Use when:** The topic requires deep, structured explanation that shorter formats cannot deliver, you want to build authority and establish thought leadership, or you are targeting an audience that is willing to invest time in learning (typically mid-to-senior professionals)

## Anatomy of a Good Guide / Ebook
- **Cover page:** Professional design with title, subtitle, author/brand, and a visual that signals the topic
- **Table of contents:** Clickable if digital, scannable if printed. Lets the reader jump to what matters most to them.
- **5-8 chapters:** Each chapter covers one subtopic completely. Chapters should build on each other but also be useful standalone.
- **Key takeaways per chapter:** A boxed or highlighted summary at the end of each chapter so skimmers still get value
- **Visuals and examples:** Charts, screenshots, case studies, or diagrams that break up text and reinforce concepts. Aim for at least one visual per 2-3 pages.
- **Conclusion with CTA:** A summary of what the reader has learned and a clear next step (book a call, try a tool, join a community)
- **Design:** Professional layout with consistent typography, branded colors, and generous whitespace. This is a reflection of your brand quality.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Outline the chapters** -- Start with the question the guide answers, then break it into 5-8 subtopics that build logically. Each chapter should have a clear premise and deliverable. Test the outline with your audience or team before writing.
2. **Write the first draft** -- Write each chapter as if it were a standalone article. Focus on practical value over theory. Include real examples, data, and specific recommendations. Aim for 1,500-3,000 words per chapter.
3. **Add visuals and examples** -- For each chapter, add at least one visual element: a chart, a screenshot, a case study, a before/after comparison, or a diagram. Visuals increase both comprehension and perceived value.
4. **Write the intro and conclusion** -- The intro should hook the reader with a problem statement and promise what they will learn. The conclusion should summarize key insights and present a compelling CTA.
5. **Edit ruthlessly** -- Cut anything that does not serve the reader. Tighten sentences. Remove filler. Have someone outside your team read it for clarity. Professional editing is worth the investment on this format.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. The title should promise a transformation or comprehensive understanding.
7. **Package and deliver** -- Design as a professional PDF with branded layout. Consider also offering a web version (landing page with chapters) for SEO value. Gate the full PDF behind email capture, but consider making the first chapter free.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]" -- e.g., *The Complete Guide to Performance Creative*
- **Formula 2:** "[Topic]: Everything [Audience] Needs to Know in [Year]" -- e.g., *UGC Advertising: Everything DTC Brands Need to Know in 2025*
- **Formula 3:** "From [Current State] to [Desired State]: A [Topic] Guide" -- e.g., *From Testing to Scaling: A Creative Strategy Guide*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 1-3 weeks (depending on depth, design quality, and editing rounds)
- **Skills needed:** Writing (long-form, educational), design (professional PDF layout), editing (structural and copy editing)
- **Maintenance:** One-time. A well-written guide has a long shelf life. Update only if the underlying topic changes significantly (e.g., major platform changes, new industry standards).
