# Swipe File Playbook

## What It Is
A swipe file is a curated collection of real-world examples -- ads, headlines, emails, designs, hooks, or any other creative asset -- organized for easy browsing and inspiration. The value is not just the examples themselves but the context and analysis that accompanies each one, turning raw inspiration into actionable insight. A great swipe file saves hours of research and competitive analysis.

## When to Use It
- **Buyer stage:** Top-of-funnel (awareness) and mid-funnel (consideration)
- **Best industries:** Agencies, creative teams, copywriters, marketers, e-commerce brands
- **Taxonomy categories:** Accelerate
- **Use when:** Your audience spends significant time researching what competitors or top performers are doing, inspiration is a bottleneck in their creative process, or you have access to a large volume of examples most people cannot easily find

## Anatomy of a Good Swipe File
- **Title with large number:** The number of examples is the primary value signal. Bigger collections command more attention (e.g., "470+" is more compelling than "50").
- **Categorization system:** Examples organized by type, industry, goal, funnel stage, or style so the user can navigate to what they need without scrolling through everything.
- **Each entry includes:**
  - The swipe content itself (screenshot, text, or link)
  - Context/analysis (1-3 sentences on why it works, what principle it uses, or what makes it stand out)
  - When to use it (the scenario or goal this example serves)
- **Search/filter capability:** For large collections, include a way to filter or search (spreadsheet filters, Notion database views, or tagged entries).
- **Regular updates:** The best swipe files grow over time, which gives you a reason to re-engage subscribers.

## Step-by-Step Creation Process
1. **Define the scope** -- Pick one specific type of creative asset to collect. "Ad creative hooks" is a swipe file. "Marketing stuff" is a junk drawer. Narrow scope increases usefulness.
2. **Set collection criteria** -- Define what qualifies for inclusion. What makes an example "swipe-worthy"? Performance data, uniqueness, clever technique, or broad applicability are good criteria. Write these down so your curation stays consistent.
3. **Collect aggressively** -- Gather far more examples than you plan to include. Pull from ad libraries, competitor research, client work (anonymized), industry tools, and your own bookmarks. Aim for 2-3x your target number.
4. **Curate and categorize** -- Cut the weakest examples. Organize what remains into clear categories. Each category should have enough entries to feel substantial (at least 5-10).
5. **Add analysis** -- For each example, write 1-3 sentences of context. What makes this work? What principle does it use? When should someone use this approach? This analysis is what separates your swipe file from a random Pinterest board.
6. **Name it** -- Apply naming conventions from naming-conventions.md. Lead with the number and be specific about the content type.
7. **Package and deliver** -- A spreadsheet or Notion database works well for large, searchable collections. A PDF works for smaller, more curated collections. Consider offering both formats.

## Naming Tips
- **Formula 1:** "[Number]+ [Adjective] [Content Type] [Assets]" -- e.g., *470+ High-Impact DTC Creative Hooks*
- **Formula 2:** "The [Audience] Swipe File: [Number] [Content Type]" -- e.g., *The Media Buyer Swipe File: 200 Proven Ad Openers*
- **Formula 3:** "[Number] [Content Type] [Assets] (Categorized & Analyzed)" -- e.g., *300 Email Subject Lines (Categorized & Analyzed)*

See naming-conventions.md for full naming guidelines.

## Linear Examples
- **470+ High-Impact DTC Creative Hooks** -- Works because the massive number (470+) signals exhaustive coverage, "high-impact" implies curation quality, and "DTC creative hooks" is a precise, high-demand niche.

## Effort Estimate
- **Time to create:** 4-8 hours for initial collection (ongoing additions over time)
- **Skills needed:** Curation (knowing what is worth including), writing (adding concise, useful analysis to each entry)
- **Maintenance:** Ongoing additions -- the best swipe files grow over time. Plan to add new entries monthly or quarterly to keep the collection fresh and give yourself re-engagement opportunities.
